Dr. Carl Sagan

The man who inspired generations to look up at the stars and embrace the infinite possibilities of the cosmos

Here's a time line of Dr. Carl Sagan:

  • 1934: Carl Sagan is born on November 9 in Brooklyn, New York, USA.
  • 1955: Graduates from the University of Chicago with a bachelor's degree in physics.
  • 1956: Receives a master's degree in physics from the University of Chicago.
  • 1960: Earns his Ph.D. in astronomy and astrophysics from the University of Chicago.
  • 1962: Publishes his first book, "Planets," co-authored with Jonathan Norton Leonard.
  • 1968: Sagan serves as a consultant to NASA and contributes to the Mariner missions to Mars and Venus.
  • 1973: Publishes "The Cosmic Connection: An Extraterrestrial Perspective," which explores the possibility of extraterrestrial life.
  • 1980: Hosts and co-writes the television series "Cosmos: A Personal Voyage," which becomes one of the most widely watched series in the history of American public television.
  • 1981: Publishes "Cosmos," the companion book to the television series, which becomes a bestseller.
  • 1985: Co-founds The Planetary Society, an organization devoted to space exploration, advocacy, and education.
  • 1994: Carl Sagan dies on December 20 in Seattle, Washington, at the age of 62, after battling myelodysplasia, a bone marrow disease.
